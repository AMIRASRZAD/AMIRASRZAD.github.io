Ph.D. Candidate in Management Information Systems

Robert J. Manning School of Business, University of Massachusetts Lowell

I am an ABD Ph.D. candidate in Business Administration (Management Information Systems) at the Robert J. Manning School of Business, University of Massachusetts Lowell, advised by Professor Xiao-Bai Li. My research focuses on Explainable AI (XAI), Interpretable Machine Learning, and Human–AI Decision Making. I study how to make AI-driven predictions more transparent, credible, and trustworthy, and how uncertainty in AI systems shapes human trust and reliance, drawing on machine learning, text mining, econometrics, and behavioral experiments.

Before starting my Ph.D., I spent ten years in industry as a business analyst and project management officer in the financial sector, which continues to inform both my research questions and my teaching in data mining, databases, and business information systems.
