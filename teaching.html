Teaching

My teaching philosophy centers on experiential, active, and project-based learning to build technical expertise, critical thinking, and the ability to work independently and in teams. Students work with real industry datasets, build predictive models using Python, SQL, Tableau, and Power BI, and complete group projects that simulate authentic business problems — demand forecasting, customer segmentation, risk assessment, and systems analysis — before presenting actionable insights to peers.

Data Mining for Business Intelligence

University of Massachusetts Lowell · Fall 2024 · Rating: 4.3/5

Undergraduate course covering the data mining pipeline for business decision-making, from data preparation through classification, clustering, and predictive modeling. Students apply techniques such as decision trees, regression, and clustering to real business datasets and build end-to-end analytics projects using Python.

Database Management Systems

University of Massachusetts Lowell · Summer 2024 (Online) · Rating: 4.9/5

Undergraduate course on relational database design, SQL, normalization, and database application development. Students design and implement a full database schema and query layer for a simulated business system, delivered entirely online with hands-on lab exercises.

Business Information Systems

University of Massachusetts Lowell · Fall 2023 – Spring 2024 · Rating: 4.2/5

Undergraduate core course introducing information systems concepts for business: systems analysis and design, enterprise systems, data-driven decision-making, and the role of IT in organizational strategy, grounded in case studies and applied projects.
